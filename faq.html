Frequently Asked Questions

The NSCET Hackathon is an innovative event designed to challenge participants to develop creative solutions to real-world problems. It encourages collaboration and problem-solving through coding and technology.

Problem statements can be submitted by various organizations, including government bodies, private companies, and educational institutions. These statements are used to guide participants in developing their solutions.

Yes, individuals working in private companies can submit problem statements. There are typically no financial charges for submitting a problem statement, but it should align with the hackathon’s objectives.

Yes, universities and institutes can submit problem statements for the hackathon. They are encouraged to propose challenges that align with their academic and research interests.

Yes, students from distance or part-time education programs are eligible to participate, provided they meet the eligibility criteria set by the hackathon organizers.

The prize money varies by hackathon. Details regarding the prize money for the NSCET Hackathon will be announced closer to the event date.

Ideas can be submitted according to the timeline specified by the hackathon organizers. Be sure to check the official schedule for submission deadlines.
